Volume 1

In the first book in the Suzuki Method, students will learn the basic technique needed to play the instrument, like bow control and division, basic sound production, intonation, A, D and G major scales/tonalities, etc. They will also develop study habits, working procedures and problem-solving skills.

How to work: General guidelines

Each difficulty in the Suzuki books is carefully planned to teach, review or develop some technical feature. The teaching points will explain in detail each of this points. Still, I will give some general guidelines to work the most common problems found in book 1, which seek to isolate the difficulty and break it in several steps:

1. Working on fingers/notes

When fingers or notes are difficult, we should practise with some known rhythm, usually Twinkle Var. A (to avoid the possible difficulty of the bow), and with pauses to prepare fingers and ensure correct coordination:

2. Working on the bow

When the bow is difficult (because we have tricky distribution, new rhythms, etc) we will practise first on open strings (to forget finger difficulty) and if possible, in Twinkle:

3. Working on String Crossings

A particular bowing issue that we can find is String Crossings. We should prepare them in open strings first (making sure we anticipate with the wrist when changing to a lower string, and with elbow when changing to a higher string). We have to work on left-hand legato as well (following the steps above, in "working on fingers/notes").

4. Working on Slurs

In order to work on slurs, the first step will be to practise the notes as in "working on fingers/notes", if needed. Then, we will play the passage with correct rhythm and notes but without slurs at all. Once this is correct, we will add slurs, first with stops in the bow (once more, to ensure correct rhythm and coordination) and finally play with slurs.
