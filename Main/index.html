Suzuki Teaching Points

Welcome to the Suzuki Teaching points website! This is a new way to present the usual teaching points required for the Suzuki Teacher Training. I hope it is interesting, intuitive, easy to use, and that the new format allows to understand, link and explain each teaching point in a different way. The web site can (and will) be edited to implement corrections, new content and ideas. Teaching points for books 2, 3, 4 and 5 will hopefully come during the next academic year (2019-2020).

How to use: General guidelines

The web is designed to be intuitive, simple, and easy to use. Still some explanation will clarify its main points. Use the menu on the left to navigate through the Suzuki Books and pieces. Each Book has its own page (as an introduction) and each piece has its page. Also, clicking on the Suzuki logo on the top left will re-direct you to this main page.

The middle section of the page has all the main content. In most cases, will be the score of the piece. Scroll to navigate through the score. Finally, the right menu will provide options to see teaching points. I decided to take advantage of the web design to elaborate a bit more each teaching idea, so we can see the structure of the piece, on the score itself; the "traditional" teaching points (the dark blue ones are preparation exercises, the light blue are teaching points that don't usually need preparation before playing the piece); right hand (for new or important features of the bow); left hand (for new or important features of the left hand); listen (plays a recording of the piece, video and different recordings will be added in the future); and last option cleans everything to see the blank score.

I hope it is clear, please let me know if you find any bugs or errors in the navigation. I have tested the page many times but the number of possible complications is huge! Thanks for reading and enjoy!
